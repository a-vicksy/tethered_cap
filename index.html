The Tethered Cap Mandate · India

This is
a love story.

Until you
opened it.

Every bottle you've ever finished ended exactly like this.

scroll

You drank. You binned it.
The bottle went off to its second life.

The cap
never made it.

Too small for the sorting machines.
It slips through every screen.

Light enough to blow out of bins,
roll into drains, ride every river.

Bottles sink and get collected.
Caps float. Caps escape.

2–3×

Caps are found in nature two to three times more often than the bottles they came from.1

To a turtle, a floating cap looks like food. Caps rank in the top five deadliest ocean plastics for marine animals.2

Volunteers have picked 7.3 million lids off the world's beaches — and they're still counting.3

The cap you lost this morning will outlive everyone you have ever met.

Now imagine
it held on.

It exists. It's called a tethered cap — it flips back, locks, and never lets go.

The EU made it the law on 3 July 2024. Coca-Cola and Pepsi already make these — for Europeans.4

Same companies.
Same bottles.
Different continent.

25,000,000,000

bottles opened in India every year. Not one is required to keep its cap.

So we took it to court. Greener Earth Foundation's petition is live at the National Green Tribunal — asking India to mandate tethered caps, the way Europe already has.
